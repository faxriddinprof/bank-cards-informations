SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION TO BANK CARDS
================================================================================

A bank card is a physical or virtual payment instrument issued by a bank or
financial institution that allows cardholders to access their funds, make
purchases, withdraw cash, and perform various financial transactions. Bank cards
are linked to a bank account and serve as the primary interface between a
customer and their money in modern banking systems.

Bank cards are embedded with a magnetic stripe, an EMV chip (microchip), and
increasingly a contactless payment antenna (NFC — Near Field Communication).
Each card carries a unique card number (typically 16 digits), an expiration
date, and a security code (CVV/CVC) used for online transactions.

In Uzbekistan, bank cards have become the dominant payment instrument for both
retail and digital commerce. The government, through the Central Bank of the
Republic of Uzbekistan, has actively promoted cashless payments, leading to
widespread adoption of local card systems such as UzCard and Humo, alongside
international systems like Visa and Mastercard.

Bank cards serve the following core purposes:
- Accessing funds stored in a bank account
- Making payments at physical stores (POS terminals)
- Withdrawing cash at ATMs
- Paying for goods and services online
- Transferring money to other individuals
- Receiving salaries, pensions, and social benefits
- Managing personal finances through mobile banking apps

The use of bank cards reduces the need to carry physical cash, provides a
transaction history for personal budgeting, and offers protection through
security features and dispute resolution mechanisms provided by banks.

In Uzbekistan, the adoption of cashless payment systems has grown significantly
since 2017, driven by government digitalization initiatives, mobile banking app
development, and QR code payment infrastructure.

SECTION 2: TYPES OF BANK CARDS
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 DEBIT CARDS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A debit card is directly linked to a customer's bank account (current account
or savings account). When a payment is made using a debit card, the funds are
immediately debited (deducted) from the linked account. The cardholder can only
spend the money that is currently available in the account unless an overdraft
facility has been enabled.

Key characteristics of debit cards:
- Funds are taken directly from the bank account balance
- No borrowing involved — the cardholder spends their own money
- Cannot be used if account balance is insufficient (without overdraft)
- Widely used for everyday purchases, ATM withdrawals, and online payments
- Issued to any account holder who meets the bank's basic eligibility criteria
- Lower risk compared to credit cards as there is no debt accumulation

In Uzbekistan, debit cards are the most common type of bank card. Most salary
cards, pension cards, and student stipend cards are debit cards. UzCard and
Humo are primarily debit card systems that operate within the national payment
infrastructure.

Common debit card types in Uzbekistan:
- Salary cards (issued through employers)
- Personal debit cards (opened individually at the bank)
- Student cards
- Pension and social benefit cards
- Children's cards (for minors with parental oversight)

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.2 CREDIT CARDS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A credit card allows the cardholder to borrow money from the issuing bank up to
a pre-approved credit limit. The borrowed amount must be repaid within a
specified period, usually monthly. If not repaid in full, interest charges
(annual percentage rate — APR) are applied to the remaining balance.

Key characteristics of credit cards:
- Spend now, pay later mechanism
- Credit limit assigned by the bank based on creditworthiness
- Grace period (interest-free period) typically ranges from 15 to 60 days
- Interest charged on outstanding balances after the grace period
- Monthly minimum payment required to avoid penalties
- Can be used for larger purchases beyond current account balance
- Builds credit history when used responsibly

In Uzbekistan, credit cards are offered by several commercial banks including:
- Kapitalbank
- Hamkorbank
- Ipoteka Bank
- TBC Bank Uzbekistan
- Anorbank

Uzbekistan credit card products often include:
- Installment cards (rassrochka cards) allowing purchases to be split into
  monthly payments without interest for a defined period
- Standard revolving credit cards with monthly statements
- Secured credit cards backed by a deposit

Credit card eligibility in Uzbekistan typically requires:
- Proof of stable income (salary certificate)
- Valid identification (passport)
- Minimum age of 18 years
- Clean credit history with the Credit Bureau of Uzbekistan
- Employment with a registered organization

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.3 VIRTUAL CARDS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A virtual card is a digital-only payment card that exists in electronic form
and does not have a physical plastic counterpart. It has all the same attributes
as a physical card — card number, expiration date, and CVV — but is accessible
only through a mobile banking application or online banking platform.

Key characteristics of virtual cards:
- No physical card — exists only in digital format
- Primarily designed for online purchases and digital payments
- Can be issued instantly through mobile banking apps
- Can be disposable (single-use) or reusable
- Enhanced security — card details can be refreshed after each transaction
- Cannot be used for ATM withdrawals (in most implementations)
- Linked to the same bank account or a separate sub-account

In Uzbekistan, virtual cards are offered by:
- Payme (via its integrated card offering)
- Click (digital wallet with virtual card features)
- Uzum Bank (formerly Apelsin) — instant virtual card issuance
- TBC Bank Uzbekistan — virtual Visa cards
- Hamkorbank — online virtual cards

Virtual cards are especially popular for:
- International online shopping (Amazon, AliExpress, etc.)
- Subscription services (Netflix, Spotify, etc.)
- Securing against online fraud — minimize exposure of main card details
- One-time purchases where users do not want to expose their primary card number

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.4 INTERNATIONAL CARDS (VISA AND MASTERCARD)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

International cards operate on global payment networks that allow transactions
to be processed in any country worldwide where the respective network is
accepted. In Uzbekistan, the two dominant international card networks are Visa
and Mastercard.

Visa International:
- One of the world's largest payment networks
- Accepted in over 200 countries and territories
- Enables online payments in foreign currencies
- Provides access to global ATM networks
- Offers Visa Zero Liability protection against unauthorized transactions
- Available in tiers: Visa Classic, Visa Gold, Visa Platinum, Visa Infinite

Mastercard International:
- Competitor to Visa, accepted in over 210 countries and territories
- Offers similar global coverage and services
- Mastercard SecureCode for online transaction authentication
- Available in tiers: Mastercard Standard, Gold, Platinum, World, World Elite

In Uzbekistan, international cards are issued by major commercial banks and are
required for:
- International travel and purchases abroad
- Online shopping on foreign websites
- Foreign currency transactions
- Access to global ATM networks (Plus, Cirrus)
- Hotel bookings, car rentals, and flight purchases

Currency conversion for international transactions:
When using an international card abroad or for foreign currency online
purchases, the transaction amount is converted from the foreign currency to
Uzbek Sum (UZS) at the exchange rate applied by the bank on the day of the
transaction. Banks may apply an additional foreign exchange markup fee
(typically 1–3%) on top of the official exchange rate.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.5 PREPAID CARDS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A prepaid card is loaded with a specific amount of money by the cardholder or
a third party before use. It is not linked to a bank account in the traditional
sense. Spending is limited to the preloaded amount.

Use cases for prepaid cards in Uzbekistan:
- Gift cards for retail promotions
- Employee benefit cards
- Travel cards loaded with a specific currency
- Cards for individuals without a bank account
- Parental allowance cards for children

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 UZCARD — NATIONAL PAYMENT SYSTEM
------------------------------------------------------------------------

UzCard is the national interbank payment card system of Uzbekistan, established
in 2004 under the oversight of the Central Bank of the Republic of Uzbekistan.
It is operated by the UzCard Processing Center and is the most widely used
payment system in the country.

Key facts about UzCard:
- Operated by: UzCard Processing Center (State Joint-Stock Company)
- Currency: Uzbek Sum (UZS) only
- Coverage: Accepted at all ATMs and POS terminals within Uzbekistan
- Card network: Domestic — does not work outside Uzbekistan
- Primary use: Salary payments, pension disbursements, local transactions
- Card number format: Starts with 8600 (standard UzCard prefix)
- Supported banks: All major commercial banks in Uzbekistan issue UzCard cards

UzCard infrastructure:
- Over 7,000 ATMs nationwide
- Over 120,000 POS terminals across retail, hospitality, and service sectors
- QR payment network via UzCard QR
- Integration with major mobile banking apps (Click, Payme, etc.)

UzCard card issuance process:
1. Customer visits a bank branch with a valid passport
2. Fills out a card application form
3. Bank opens a current account linked to the card
4. Card is issued immediately or within 1–5 business days
5. PIN code is set at the branch or via ATM/mobile app

UzCard card types:
- UzCard Standard (basic debit card)
- UzCard Gold (premium benefits, higher limits)
- UzCard Paywave (contactless payment enabled)
- Co-branded cards (issued in partnership with retailers or employers)

UzCard security features:
- EMV chip technology
- PIN-based authentication at ATMs and POS
- SMS OTP for mobile banking operations
- 3D Secure for online transactions (where supported)

UzCard transaction fees (general):
- Inter-bank transfers between UzCard accounts: 0.5–1% fee (varies by bank)
- ATM withdrawals from own bank: Free
- ATM withdrawals from other banks: 0.5–1% fee
- POS payments: No fee for the cardholder (merchant pays the processing fee)

UzCard customer support: 1243 (short number, toll-free within Uzbekistan)

3.2 HUMO — NATIONAL PAYMENT SYSTEM
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Humo is the second national payment card system of Uzbekistan, launched in
2019. It was developed as an alternative to UzCard to increase competition and

Interbank Processing Center (NIPC).

Key facts about Humo:
- Operated by: National Interbank Processing Center (NIPC)
- Currency: Uzbek Sum (UZS) only
- Coverage: Accepted at ATMs and POS terminals within Uzbekistan
- Card network: Domestic — does not work outside Uzbekistan
- Card number format: Starts with 9860 (standard Humo prefix)
- Supported banks: All major commercial banks in Uzbekistan also issue Humo

Differences between UzCard and Humo:
- Both are domestic card systems operating only within Uzbekistan
- Humo introduced more modern infrastructure with real-time processing
- Humo cards are issued by a slightly different set of partner banks
- Both systems are interoperable at ATMs and POS terminals as of 2021
- Some banks issue combo cards that function as both UzCard and Humo

Humo card types:
- Humo Standard
- Humo Gold
- Humo Virtual (for online payments)
- Humo Paywave (contactless)

Humo security features:
- EMV chip
- PIN authentication
- SMS notifications for all transactions
- Fraud monitoring system

Humo customer support: 1209 (short number, toll-free within Uzbekistan)

SECTION 4: CARD OPERATIONS
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 CARD ACTIVATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

When a new bank card is issued, it is typically in an inactive state for
security purposes. The cardholder must activate the card before first use.

Methods to activate a card in Uzbekistan:

Method 1 — ATM Activation:
1. Insert the new card into any ATM of the issuing bank
2. Enter the PIN code provided with the card or set a new one
3. The system automatically activates the card upon successful PIN entry
4. Confirm activation via SMS notification

Method 2 — Mobile Banking App:
1. Log in to the bank's official mobile banking application
2. Navigate to "Cards" or "My Cards" section
3. Select the newly issued card
4. Click "Activate Card" button
5. Confirm identity via OTP (One-Time Password) sent to registered mobile number
6. Card is activated instantly

Method 3 — Bank Branch:
1. Visit the issuing bank branch with a valid passport
2. Request card activation from a bank teller
3. Provide proof of identity
4. Card is activated within minutes

Method 4 — Phone Banking (Call Center):
1. Call the bank's official customer support number
2. Verify identity through security questions and registered phone number
3. Request card activation
4. Receive confirmation via SMS

Important note: Cards that are not activated within 30–90 days of issuance
(depending on bank policy) may be deactivated or returned. Always activate
your card promptly upon receipt.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.2 PIN CODE USAGE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A PIN (Personal Identification Number) is a 4-digit or 6-digit secret code
used to authenticate the cardholder during ATM withdrawals, POS payments, and
certain online transactions.

PIN code rules and best practices:
- Never share your PIN with anyone, including bank employees
- Memorize your PIN — do not write it on the card or store it in your wallet
- Change your PIN regularly for security
- Avoid easily guessable PINs (1234, 0000, birth year)
- Always shield the keypad when entering your PIN at ATMs or POS terminals

PIN code entry at ATM:
- Insert card → Enter PIN → Access account menu
- 3 consecutive wrong PIN attempts → Card is temporarily blocked
- Blocked card can be unblocked at the bank branch or via mobile app

PIN code at POS terminal:
- Contactless payments below a threshold (usually 200,000–500,000 UZS)
  may not require PIN
- Larger contactless or chip payments require PIN entry
- Some merchants may require signature instead of PIN for international cards

Setting or changing PIN:
Method 1 — ATM: Insert card → Select "PIN Management" → Enter current PIN →
Enter new PIN → Confirm new PIN → Done
Method 2 — Mobile Banking App: Cards section → Select card →
"Change PIN" → Follow instructions → Confirm via OTP
Method 3 — Bank Branch: Visit with passport → Request PIN change/reset

Forgotten PIN procedure:
1. Visit the nearest branch of the issuing bank
2. Bring a valid passport (or equivalent identification)
3. Request PIN reset
4. Bank resets PIN — cardholder sets a new PIN at ATM or in mobile app
5. Alternatively, call the bank's call center and complete identity verification

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.3 ATM WITHDRAWALS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

ATM (Automated Teller Machine) withdrawals allow cardholders to access cash
from their bank account using their debit or credit card.

Step-by-step ATM withdrawal process:
1. Insert your card into the card slot (chip end first, face up)
2. Select language if prompted
3. Enter your 4-digit (or 6-digit) PIN code
4. Select "Cash Withdrawal" or "Nalichnie" from the menu
5. Enter the desired withdrawal amount
6. Confirm the transaction
7. Collect cash from the dispenser
8. Collect your card (card is returned before cash is dispensed on most ATMs)
9. Collect your receipt (optional)

ATM withdrawal tips:
- Verify the ATM is official and not tampered with before use
- Check for card skimming devices on the card slot
- Use ATMs in well-lit, secure locations
- Never accept help from strangers while using an ATM
- Cancel the transaction if anything seems suspicious

ATM withdrawal limits (approximate for Uzbekistan):
- UzCard/Humo: Daily limit typically 10,000,000–20,000,000 UZS per day
- International cards (Visa/MC): Daily limit in foreign currency typically
  $500–$2,000 equivalent
- Single transaction maximum: Usually 5,000,000 UZS for domestic cards
- Some premium cards have higher limits
(Exact limits depend on the issuing bank and card type)

Common ATM errors and meanings:
- "Insufficient funds": Account balance is lower than the withdrawal amount
- "Card blocked": Card has been blocked due to security reasons or wrong PIN
- "Transaction limit exceeded": Daily or per-transaction limit has been reached
- "ATM out of cash": The ATM's cash cassette is empty
- "Card not supported": The ATM does not accept the card network type
- "System error": Temporary technical issue — try another ATM

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.4 POS PAYMENTS (POINT OF SALE)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

POS (Point of Sale) payments are made using a bank card at a merchant's payment
terminal. This includes payments at supermarkets, restaurants, clothing stores,
pharmacies, and any other business that accepts card payments.

Types of POS payments:

Chip and PIN payment:
1. Cardholder presents card to cashier
2. Cashier enters the payment amount into the terminal
3. Customer inserts the card into the chip reader
4. Customer enters PIN on the keypad
5. Terminal processes the transaction
6. Approval message is displayed
7. Transaction is complete — receipt may be printed

Contactless payment (NFC/Paywave):
1. Customer holds the card close to the contactless reader (within 4 cm)
2. Terminal emits a beep or visual signal upon successful read
3. For amounts below the contactless limit (usually 200,000–500,000 UZS):
   No PIN required
4. For amounts above the contactless limit: PIN required
5. Transaction completes in under 1 second

Mobile payment via NFC (digital wallet):
- Cardholders can add cards to Payme, Click, or Uzum apps
- Phone is held near the contactless reader
- Authenticate with fingerprint, face recognition, or PIN on phone
- Payment completes without physical card

POS payment tips:
- Never let the card leave your sight during a payment
- Verify the amount on the terminal before entering PIN
- Always take your receipt for large purchases
- If the terminal is slow or the clerk seems suspicious, cancel the transaction

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4.5 ONLINE PAYMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Online payments involve using card details to pay for goods or services on
websites, apps, or digital platforms.

Information required for online payment:
- Card number (16-digit number embossed on the front)
- Expiration date (MM/YY format, found on the front of the card)
- CVV/CVC code (3-digit security code on the back of the card)
- Cardholder name (as printed on the card)
- Billing address (for some international merchants)

3D Secure authentication:
- 3D Secure (3DS) is an additional security layer for online payments
- When a 3DS-enrolled card is used online, an OTP (One-Time Password) is
  sent to the cardholder's registered phone number
- The cardholder must enter this OTP on the payment page within a time limit
  (usually 3–5 minutes)
- If OTP is not entered correctly or times out, the payment is declined
- This prevents unauthorized use of card details for online purchases

Online payment platforms widely used in Uzbekistan:
- Payme (payme.uz) — widely used for utility bills, mobile top-up, e-commerce
- Click (click.uz) — similar multi-purpose payment platform
- Uzum Market — e-commerce platform with integrated card payment
- Apelsin (now Uzum Bank app) — mobile payments
- OLX Uzbekistan — peer-to-peer marketplace with card payments

Security tips for online payments:
- Only shop on HTTPS-secured websites
- Verify the merchant's legitimacy before entering card details
- Use virtual cards for online purchases to limit exposure
- Never enter card details on links received via SMS or email
- Enable transaction notifications to monitor all card activity

SECTION 5: TRANSACTIONS
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 SUCCESSFUL TRANSACTION FLOW
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A standard card transaction follows this technical flow:

Step 1 — Authorization Request:
The merchant's payment terminal or online payment gateway sends an authorization
request to the card network (UzCard, Humo, Visa, Mastercard).

Step 2 — Network Routing:
The card network routes the request to the cardholder's issuing bank.

Step 3 — Issuer Verification:
The issuing bank checks:
- Card validity (not expired, not blocked)
- Account balance (sufficient funds for debit card)
- Credit limit (for credit cards)
- Transaction limits (daily/per-transaction)
- Fraud detection rules

Step 4 — Authorization Response:
The bank sends an approval or denial response back through the network to the
merchant's terminal.

Step 5 — Settlement:
At end of day, the merchant's bank (acquirer) batches and submits all
transactions for settlement. Funds are transferred from cardholder's account
to the merchant's account. This may take 1–3 business days.

Step 6 — Statement:
The transaction appears on the cardholder's account statement. For mobile
banking users, an instant SMS/push notification is sent.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.2 FAILED TRANSACTION REASONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A transaction can fail at various stages. Common reasons include:

Insufficient funds:
- The account balance is lower than the transaction amount
- Solution: Add funds to the account or use a different payment method

Card blocked or frozen:
- Card has been blocked due to security concerns, wrong PIN attempts, or by
  the cardholder themselves
- Solution: Contact the bank to unblock the card

Expired card:
- The card's expiration date has passed
- Solution: Request a card replacement from the bank

Transaction limit exceeded:
- The transaction amount or daily cumulative amount exceeds the set limit
- Solution: Contact the bank to increase limits or split the purchase

Incorrect card details (online payments):
- Card number, expiration date, or CVV entered incorrectly
- Solution: Double-check and re-enter card details

3D Secure failure:
- OTP was not entered, was entered incorrectly, or expired
- Solution: Retry the payment and enter the OTP promptly

Network or system error:
- Technical issues with the payment network or bank systems
- Solution: Wait a few minutes and retry, or use a different payment method

Card not enabled for online transactions:
- Some cards are not enabled by default for online or international payments
- Solution: Enable online payments via mobile banking app or by contacting the bank

Merchant restrictions:
- Some merchants do not accept certain card types or networks
- Solution: Use an accepted card type or payment method

Fraud detection block:
- The bank's fraud detection system flagged the transaction as suspicious
- This often happens for unusual spending patterns, foreign transactions,
  or large amounts
- Solution: Contact the bank to confirm the transaction and unblock

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.3 PENDING TRANSACTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A pending transaction is a transaction that has been authorized but not yet
fully settled and cleared. The amount is reserved (held) from the available
balance but has not yet been permanently deducted.

Pending transactions occur when:
- A purchase has been authorized but the merchant has not yet completed
  settlement (common in hotels, car rentals, and restaurants)
- A refund has been initiated but not yet processed
- An online transaction is awaiting 3D Secure confirmation
- A pre-authorization hold has been placed (e.g., hotel check-in deposit)

Pending transaction duration:
- Typically resolves within 1–5 business days
- If a pending transaction does not clear, the hold is released and funds
  become available again (usually within 7 business days)

Pre-authorization holds:
Hotels, car rental companies, and some online merchants place a temporary
hold (pre-authorization) on the card to ensure funds are available. This is
not an actual charge but reduces available balance temporarily.
- Hotel check-in hold: Typically $50–$200 or equivalent
- Car rental hold: Typically $200–$500 or equivalent
- The hold is released when the actual charge is processed upon checkout

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.4 REFUND PROCESS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A refund returns previously paid funds back to the cardholder's account.
Refunds occur when:
- A purchase is returned to the merchant
- A transaction was made in error
- A service was not delivered as expected
- A duplicate charge occurred

Standard refund process:
1. Customer requests a refund from the merchant (in-store or online)
2. Merchant initiates a refund transaction on their payment terminal/system
3. Refund is processed through the card network
4. Issuing bank receives the refund credit
5. Funds are credited back to the cardholder's account

Refund timeline:
- Domestic cards (UzCard, Humo): 1–5 business days
- International cards (Visa, Mastercard): 5–10 business days (sometimes longer
  for cross-border transactions)
- Online refunds: May take up to 14 business days depending on the merchant

Important notes on refunds:
- Refunds are always credited back to the original card used for payment
- If the original card has been cancelled or replaced, the refund is still
  directed to the account — contact the bank if the new card number is different
- Cash refunds from a card purchase are generally not provided by merchants;
  refunds are always processed as card reversals
- Some merchants may offer store credit instead of a card refund

Disputing a transaction:
If a customer believes a transaction is incorrect, unauthorized, or fraudulent:
1. Contact the issuing bank's call center immediately
2. Provide transaction details (date, amount, merchant name)
3. Bank opens a dispute/chargeback investigation
4. During investigation, a provisional credit may be applied to the account
5. Investigation typically takes 15–45 business days
6. If dispute is resolved in favor of the cardholder, the amount is permanently
   credited; if in favor of the merchant, the provisional credit is reversed

SECTION 6: SECURITY
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1 FRAUD TYPES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

PHISHING:
Phishing is a social engineering attack where criminals send fake
emails, SMS messages, or create fake websites that appear to be from a
legitimate bank or payment service. The goal is to trick the cardholder into
revealing sensitive information such as card numbers, PINs, OTPs, or passwords.

How phishing works:
- Fraudster sends an SMS or email claiming to be from the cardholder's bank
- Message contains a sense of urgency (e.g., "Your card will be blocked")
- Message includes a link to a fake banking website
- Cardholder enters their credentials on the fake site
- Fraudster captures the information and uses it for unauthorized transactions

How to recognize phishing:
- Messages asking for your card number, PIN, or OTP are always fraudulent
  (legitimate banks NEVER ask for these via SMS, email, or phone)
- Sender email address does not match the official bank domain
- URL of website has misspellings or is not the official bank URL
- Message contains poor grammar or spelling
- Urgency tactics ("act immediately or your account will be suspended")

CARD SKIMMING:
Card skimming is the physical theft of card data using devices installed on
ATMs or POS terminals. A skimming device reads and copies the magnetic stripe
data from the card. A hidden camera or false keypad overlay also captures the PIN.

How to detect skimming:
- Check the card reader for any unusual attachments or loose parts
- Wiggle the card slot — a legitimate slot should be firm and secure
- Look for an unusual keypad overlay or small cameras above the keypad
- Report suspicious ATMs to the bank immediately
- Prefer ATMs located inside bank branches or well-monitored areas

SIM SWAP FRAUD:
SIM swap occurs when a criminal convinces the mobile operator to transfer the
victim's phone number to a new SIM card controlled by the fraudster. Once the
fraudster controls the victim's phone number, they can receive OTPs and access
mobile banking apps.

How SIM swap works:
1. Fraudster gathers personal information about the target (via phishing or
   data breaches)
2. Fraudster contacts the mobile operator pretending to be the victim
3. Requests a SIM replacement claiming phone is lost or damaged
4. New SIM is issued to the fraudster
5. Fraudster receives all OTPs and can reset banking passwords

Signs of SIM swap attack:
- Sudden loss of mobile signal without apparent reason
- Unable to make or receive calls despite good network coverage
- SMS messages stop arriving
- Mobile banking app shows unusual activity

Prevention:
- Contact your mobile operator immediately if you lose mobile service unexpectedly
- Enable additional security on your mobile account (PIN with operator)
- Use authenticator apps instead of SMS for 2FA where possible

CARDING (CARD DATA THEFT):
Carding refers to the use of stolen card data (obtained through data breaches,
phishing, or dark web purchases) to make fraudulent purchases. The fraudster
does not have the physical card but possesses the card details.

MAN-IN-THE-MIDDLE ATTACKS:
An attacker intercepts communication between the cardholder and the bank
system, typically on unsecured Wi-Fi networks, to capture transaction data.
Prevention: Avoid using online banking or making card payments on public
or unsecured Wi-Fi networks. Use a VPN when on public networks.

COUNTERFEIT CARDS:
Using skimmed or stolen card data, criminals create physical counterfeit
cards with the magnetic stripe programmed with stolen data. EMV chip cards
have largely reduced this threat for chip transactions, but magnetic stripe
transactions remain vulnerable.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.2 HOW TO DETECT FRAUD
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Monitoring your account:
- Enable SMS and push notifications for all transactions
- Check your account statement regularly (at least weekly)
- Review notifications immediately after each transaction to verify accuracy
- Report any unfamiliar or unexpected transaction immediately

Signs of potential fraud on your account:
- Transactions you do not recognize
- Small test transactions (fraudsters often make small charges first to verify
  a stolen card is active)
- Foreign transactions you did not make
- Account balance lower than expected
- Receiving OTPs you did not request
- Being locked out of your mobile banking app

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.3 WHAT TO DO IF FRAUD HAPPENS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Immediate actions:
1. BLOCK YOUR CARD IMMEDIATELY via mobile banking app, ATM, or call center
2. Call your bank's 24/7 fraud hotline
3. Change your online banking password and PIN immediately
4. Report the unauthorized transactions to the bank
5. If SIM swap is suspected, contact your mobile operator to secure your account
6. File a complaint with the bank for each unauthorized transaction
7. If a significant amount was lost, file a police report

Information to provide to the bank:
- Your full name and account number
- Card number (or last 4 digits)
- Description of unauthorized transactions (date, amount, merchant if known)
- When you first noticed the fraud
- Any suspicious communications you received (SMS, email, calls)

Chargeback rights:
- Cardholders have the right to dispute unauthorized transactions
- Visa and Mastercard have chargeback rules that protect consumers
- Disputes must typically be filed within 60–120 days of the transaction date
- The bank investigates and, if fraud is confirmed, the amount is refunded

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.4 CARD BLOCKING PROCEDURES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

A card can be blocked (frozen) through several methods:

Self-blocking via Mobile Banking App:
1. Open the bank's official mobile banking app
2. Navigate to "Cards" section
3. Select the card to block
4. Tap "Block Card" or "Freeze Card"
5. Select reason for blocking (lost, stolen, security concern, temporary)
6. Confirm the action via OTP or biometric authentication
7. Card is blocked immediately

Blocking via ATM:
1. Insert card at an ATM
2. Select "Card Management" or "Services" option
3. Choose "Block Card"
4. Enter PIN to confirm
5. Card is blocked

Blocking via Call Center:
1. Call the bank's 24/7 customer support number
2. Verify identity (full name, passport number, secret word, last transaction)
3. Request card blocking
4. Bank blocks the card immediately
5. Reference number is provided for the complaint

Blocking via Bank Branch:
1. Visit the nearest branch with valid ID
2. Fill out a card blocking request form
3. Bank blocks the card in the system

Temporary block vs. permanent block:
- Temporary block: Card is frozen but not cancelled. Can be unblocked later.
  Used when card is misplaced but not confirmed stolen.
- Permanent block: Card is permanently cancelled. A new card must be issued.
  Used when card is confirmed stolen, compromised, or lost permanently.

Unblocking a temporarily blocked card:
- Via mobile banking app: Cards → Select card → Unblock Card → Confirm with OTP
- Via call center: Verify identity → Request unblock
- Via bank branch: Present ID and request unblock

After permanent block:
- Request a new card reissuance from the bank
- Provide identification
- New card issued with new card number
- Update stored card details on any saved payment methods (apps, websites)

SECTION 8: PROBLEMS AND SOLUTIONS
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM 1: CARD NOT WORKING AT ATM
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Customer inserts card at an ATM but the card is rejected or
the transaction cannot be completed.

Possible Causes:
a) Card is blocked (wrong PIN entered 3+ times, security block, self-blocked)
b) Card is expired (expiration date has passed)
c) Card is damaged (chip or magnetic stripe is scratched or damaged)
d) ATM does not support the card network (e.g., Visa card at a UzCard-only ATM)
e) ATM is out of cash or has a technical malfunction
f) Card has been demagnetized (stored near magnetic sources)
g) Daily withdrawal limit has been reached
h) Account balance is zero or insufficient

Solutions:
a) Check if card is blocked via mobile banking app; unblock if possible or call
   the bank. Verify remaining PIN attempts.
b) Request card renewal at the bank branch.
c) Visit the bank to request a card replacement.
d) Try a different ATM that supports your card network.
e) Try a different ATM or return later.
f) Keep card away from magnets; request replacement if demagnetized.
g) Check daily limit via mobile app; wait until the next calendar day.
h) Add funds to the account.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM 2: MONEY DEDUCTED BUT PAYMENT FAILED
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Customer pays at a POS terminal or online, the amount is
deducted from the balance, but the merchant confirms the payment was not received
or the order was not completed.

Possible Causes:
a) Network communication error between merchant terminal and bank
b) Pending authorization that has not cleared
c) Double transaction (duplicate charge)
d) Online payment: 3D Secure OTP timed out but initial authorization went through
e) Refund processing delay

Solutions:
a) Check account statement — if transaction appears as "pending," wait 3–5 days.
   If not resolved, contact the bank with the transaction reference.
b) Wait for the pending transaction to clear or reverse (usually within 7 days).
c) Report to bank to initiate a duplicate charge dispute.
d) Contact the merchant to confirm order status; if order was not placed, contact
   the bank to reverse the authorization.
e) Request a refund from the merchant; if they confirm the refund has been
   processed, wait up to 14 business days for it to appear.

Important: Always keep payment receipts, screenshots of error messages, and
order confirmation numbers when contacting the bank or merchant.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM 3: PIN CODE FORGOTTEN
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Customer cannot remember their PIN and has been locked out.

Possible Causes:
- Customer forgot the PIN
- Card may be temporarily blocked after 3 wrong PIN attempts

Solutions:
1. Do NOT attempt more PIN entries if you are unsure — 3 wrong attempts will
   block the card.
2. Visit the nearest bank branch with a valid passport.
3. Request PIN reset.
4. Bank resets the PIN and the customer sets a new PIN at the ATM or via mobile app.
5. Alternatively, contact the call center to initiate PIN reset (may require
   identity verification and a branch visit for security).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM 4: CARD LOST OR STOLEN
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Customer cannot locate their card or suspects it has been stolen.

Immediate Actions:
1. Block the card immediately via:
   - Mobile banking app (fastest method)
   - Call center 24/7 hotline
   - Bank branch
2. Review recent transactions for unauthorized charges.
3. Report unauthorized transactions to the bank.
4. File a police report if the card was stolen (required for some chargeback cases).
5. Request a new card from the bank.
6. Update saved card details in any apps or websites.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM 5: CARD BLOCKED FOR UNKNOWN REASON
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Customer's card stops working unexpectedly without them blocking it.

Possible Causes:
a) Bank's fraud detection system flagged a transaction
b) Card expired and was automatically deactivated
c) Account has been frozen due to legal or compliance reasons
d) Multiple failed PIN attempts by someone else (possible theft attempt)
e) Bank sent a security notification and blocked pending customer response

Solutions:
a) Call the bank's customer service to inquire about the reason. Confirm that
   recent transactions were genuine.
b) Check the expiration date on the card. If expired, request renewal.
c) Contact the bank to understand the reason and resolve account issues.
d) Change PIN immediately and monitor for unauthorized activity.
e) Respond to the bank's security query and verify transactions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM 6: ONLINE PAYMENT DECLINED DESPITE SUFFICIENT BALANCE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Customer has enough balance but online payment is being declined.

Possible Causes:
a) Online payments not enabled on the card
b) 3D Secure not set up or OTP not received
c) Incorrect CVV or card details entered
d) Online payment daily limit reached
e) Merchant does not accept the card network
f) Browser or device has a security issue blocking the payment

Solutions:
a) Enable online payments via mobile banking app under card settings.
b) Ensure mobile number registered with the bank is active and can receive SMS.
   Contact the bank if OTP is not arriving.
c) Double-check all card details entered on the payment page.
d) Check online payment limit via mobile app; contact bank to increase.
e) Check if the merchant's accepted payment methods list includes your card type.
f) Try a different browser, clear cache, disable VPN, or try another device.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM 7: TRANSACTION NOT APPEARING IN STATEMENT
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Customer performed a transaction but it does not appear in their
account statement or mobile banking transaction history.

Possible Causes:
a) Transaction is still pending (authorized but not settled)
b) Settlement delay (especially on weekends or public holidays)
c) Date filter in mobile app not showing the transaction date correctly
d) Transaction was declined and no debit occurred

Solutions:
a) Check the "pending transactions" section in mobile app.
b) Wait 2–3 business days for the transaction to appear.
c) Adjust date range filter in the mobile banking app's transaction history.
d) Verify if funds were actually deducted from the balance.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM 8: CARD EXPIRATION AND RENEWAL
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Customer's card is nearing or has passed its expiration date.

Card expiration details:
- Bank cards typically have a validity period of 2–5 years
- Expiration date is printed as MM/YY on the front of the card
- The card expires at the end of the printed month (e.g., 05/26 expires
  at the end of May 2026)

Renewal process:
1. Bank usually sends SMS notification 30–60 days before expiration
2. Customer visits bank branch to request a new card
3. New card is issued with a new expiration date (card number may remain
   the same or change, depending on bank policy)
4. Old card should be destroyed (cut up) after new card is activated
5. Update card details on any saved payment methods

Automatic renewal:
Some banks automatically issue a renewed card and mail it or notify the customer
to collect from the branch. Contact your bank for their specific renewal policy.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM 9: SMS NOTIFICATION NOT RECEIVED
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Customer is not receiving SMS notifications for transactions.

Possible Causes:
a) Mobile number changed and not updated at the bank
b) Mobile operator network issue or SMS spam filter
c) SMS notification service disabled in card settings
d) Account balance is zero and SMS subscription has lapsed

Solutions:
a) Visit the bank to update the registered mobile number.
b) Check if SMS from the bank number is blocked; contact mobile operator.
c) Enable SMS notifications in mobile banking app under notification settings.
d) Top up account to cover SMS subscription fee if applicable.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM 10: WRONG TRANSFER AMOUNT OR WRONG RECIPIENT
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Description: Customer transferred money to the wrong card number or entered
the incorrect amount.

Solutions:
1. Contact the bank IMMEDIATELY via call center.
2. Provide details of the transfer (amount, recipient card number, time).
3. Bank may be able to reverse the transfer if the recipient account has
   not been depleted.
4. If funds cannot be reversed automatically, the bank may attempt to contact
   the recipient's bank.
5. For legally recovering funds transferred to a wrong recipient, the customer
   may need to file a formal written complaint and, in some cases, legal action.

Note: Banks are not legally obligated to guarantee recovery of funds sent to
the wrong account. Exercise extreme care when entering recipient card numbers.
Always double-check the recipient's name displayed after entering a card number
before confirming a transfer.

SECTION 10: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
GENERAL CARD QUESTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q1: What is a bank card?
A: A bank card is a payment card issued by a bank that allows you to access
your funds, make purchases, withdraw cash at ATMs, and pay online. It is linked
to your bank account.

Q2: What is the difference between a debit card and a credit card?
A: A debit card uses your own money from your bank account. A credit card lets
you borrow money from the bank up to a credit limit, which you repay later.

Q3: What is UzCard?
A: UzCard is Uzbekistan's national payment card system operated by the UzCard
Processing Center. It works only within Uzbekistan and is the most widely used
card system in the country for domestic transactions.

Q4: What is Humo?
A: Humo is Uzbekistan's second national payment card system, launched in 2019
and operated by the National Interbank Processing Center (NIPC). It works only
within Uzbekistan and is accepted at most ATMs and POS terminals nationwide.

Q5: Can I use my UzCard or Humo card outside Uzbekistan?
A: No. UzCard and Humo are domestic payment systems and only work within
Uzbekistan. For international transactions or travel abroad, you need a
Visa or Mastercard issued by a Uzbekistan bank.

Q6: How do I get a bank card in Uzbekistan?
A: Visit any branch of a commercial bank with your passport. Complete the card
application form, provide required documents, and a card will be issued to you
immediately or within a few business days.

Q7: What is a CVV/CVC code?
A: It is a 3-digit security code printed on the back of your card (on the
signature strip). It is used as additional verification for online and
phone-based transactions. Never share this code with anyone.

Q8: What is a virtual card?
A: A virtual card is a digital-only card with no physical form. It exists in a
mobile banking app and is used for online purchases. It provides extra security
as it keeps your main card details safe.

Q9: What is a contactless payment?
A: Contactless payment allows you to pay by simply tapping or holding your card
near a payment terminal's contactless reader. No card insertion or PIN is
required for small amounts.

Q10: What is NFC payment?
A: NFC (Near Field Communication) is the technology that enables contactless
payments. It allows your card or smartphone to communicate wirelessly with the
payment terminal when held nearby.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PIN AND SECURITY QUESTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q11: How many times can I enter a wrong PIN before my card is blocked?
A: Typically 3 consecutive wrong PIN entries will temporarily block your card.
The exact number may vary slightly by bank, but 3 is the standard.

Q12: How do I change my PIN?
A: You can change your PIN at any ATM of the issuing bank, through the bank's
mobile banking app, or by visiting a bank branch.

Q13: Can a bank employee tell me my PIN?
A: No. Bank employees cannot access your PIN. If anyone claiming to be from
your bank asks for your PIN, it is a fraud attempt. Report it immediately.

Q14: What should I do if I suspect someone knows my PIN?
A: Change your PIN immediately via ATM or mobile app. Check your recent
transactions for any unauthorized activity. Contact your bank if you spot
anything suspicious.

Q15: What is 3D Secure?
A: 3D Secure is a security protocol for online payments. When you make an
online purchase, a one-time password (OTP) is sent to your registered phone
number to verify that you are the authorized cardholder.

Q16: How can I protect my card from fraud?
A: Never share your card number, PIN, CVV, or OTP with anyone. Use ATMs in
secure locations. Monitor transactions regularly. Enable SMS notifications.
Use 3D Secure for online purchases. Report lost or stolen cards immediately.

Q17: What is card skimming?
A: Skimming is when criminals attach a device to an ATM or POS terminal to
steal your card's magnetic stripe data and capture your PIN. Always check for
unusual attachments on ATMs before using them.

Q18: What is phishing?
A: Phishing is when criminals send fake emails, messages, or create fake
websites to trick you into providing your card details. Always verify you are
on the official website and never click on suspicious links.

Q19: What is a SIM swap attack?
A: A SIM swap attack is when a criminal convinces your mobile operator to
transfer your phone number to a new SIM they control. They can then receive
your OTPs. If you suddenly lose mobile service, contact your operator immediately.

Q20: Can I use my card on public Wi-Fi?
A: It is not recommended. Public Wi-Fi networks can be insecure. Avoid
entering card details or accessing mobile banking on unsecured public Wi-Fi.
Use mobile data or a VPN instead.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
ATM QUESTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q21: What is the maximum amount I can withdraw from an ATM per day?
A: Limits vary by card type and bank. For standard UzCard/Humo cards, the
typical daily limit is 10,000,000 UZS. Contact your bank for the exact limit
on your card.

Q22: My card was swallowed by the ATM. What do I do?
A: Do not leave the ATM. Note the ATM's location and ID number (usually
printed on the machine). Call your bank immediately. The card can be retrieved
or cancelled and replaced.

Q23: The ATM dispensed less cash than I requested. What do I do?
A: Do not leave the ATM. Note the discrepancy and contact your bank immediately.
ATM machines are reconciled daily and any dispensing errors are correctable
through a formal complaint.

Q24: Can I use any ATM to withdraw from my UzCard account?
A: You can use any ATM in Uzbekistan that is on the UzCard network. Using your
own bank's ATMs is usually free; using other banks' ATMs may incur a small fee.

Q25: My ATM receipt shows a different amount than what was dispensed. What do I do?
A: Contact your bank immediately with the receipt and details of the
discrepancy. The bank will investigate and correct the error if confirmed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
ONLINE PAYMENT QUESTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q26: How do I enable online payments on my card?
A: Log in to your mobile banking app, go to Cards, select your card, and
find the Online Payments setting to enable it. Alternatively, call your bank.

Q27: My OTP is not arriving. What should I do?
A: Ensure your mobile number is registered with the bank and is active. Check
that SMS messages are not blocked. If the problem persists, contact your bank.

Q28: Is it safe to save my card details on shopping websites?
A: Use caution. Only save card details on reputable, secure websites (HTTPS).
Consider using a virtual card for online shopping to protect your main card.

Q29: What is a payment gateway?
A: A payment gateway is a technology that securely processes card payment
information between the customer, merchant, and the banks during an online
transaction.

Q30: Can I use Payme or Click without a bank card?
A: Payme and Click require a linked UzCard or Humo card to fund the wallet.
However, once funded, you can transact within the platform using the wallet balance.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
TRANSACTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q31: How long does a bank transfer take?
A: Transfers between accounts at the same bank (intra-bank) are typically
instant or within a few minutes. Transfers between different banks
(inter-bank) within Uzbekistan take 1 business day. International transfers
typically take 3–5 business days.

Q32: I sent money to the wrong account. Can it be reversed?
A: Contact your bank immediately. Recovery is not guaranteed, but the bank
will attempt to contact the receiving bank to retrieve the funds. Act quickly.

Q33: Why is my transaction showing as "pending"?
A: A pending transaction has been authorized but not yet settled. This is
normal and usually resolves within 1–5 business days.

Q34: How long does a refund take to appear on my account?
A: For domestic card transactions, refunds typically appear within 1–5
business days. For international transactions, it may take 5–14 business days.

Q35: Can I cancel a card payment that I just made?
A: You cannot cancel a completed payment directly. You must request a refund
from the merchant. If the merchant refuses and the charge was unauthorized,
you can file a chargeback with your bank.

Q36: What is a chargeback?
A: A chargeback is a forced reversal of a card transaction initiated by the
cardholder's bank on behalf of the cardholder when a transaction is disputed.
It is a consumer protection mechanism.

Q37: How do I check my transaction history?
A: You can view your transaction history in your bank's mobile app,
online banking portal, or by requesting a statement at the bank branch.

Q38: What is a transaction reference number?
A: It is a unique code assigned to each transaction for tracking and
identification purposes. Always note this number when disputing a transaction.

CARD MANAGEMENT QUESTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q39: How long does it take to get a new card?
A: Standard card issuance typically takes 1–5 business days. Some banks offer
instant card issuance at the branch. Express cards may be issued within the
same day for an additional fee.

Q40: How do I activate my new card?
A: You can activate your card at any ATM of the issuing bank by inserting it
and entering your PIN, through the mobile banking app, or by calling the bank.

Q41: What is the validity period of a bank card?
A: Most bank cards in Uzbekistan are valid for 2–5 years. The expiration date
is printed on the front of the card in MM/YY format.

Q42: What happens when my card expires?
A: Contact your bank before expiry to request a new card. The bank will issue
a new card with a new expiration date. You should activate the new card and
destroy the old one.

Q43: Can I have multiple cards linked to one account?
A: Yes. Banks typically allow additional (supplementary) cards to be issued
on the same account, often for family members. These share the same account
balance with individual card limits available.

Q44: Can I temporarily freeze my card without cancelling it?
A: Yes. Most banks allow you to temporarily freeze (block) your card through
the mobile banking app. The card can be unfrozen at any time without needing
to issue a new card.

Q45: How do I close/cancel my card?
A: Visit the bank branch with your passport and fill out a card closure form.
Any outstanding balance must be cleared. Return or destroy the physical card.

FEES AND LIMITS QUESTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q46: Is there a fee for using my card at POS terminals?
A: Generally, no. POS payments do not incur fees for the cardholder in
Uzbekistan. The merchant pays the processing fee.

Q47: Are there fees for mobile banking transfers?
A: Yes, typically. Transfers between different banks usually carry a fee of
0.5–1% of the amount. Transfers within the same bank are often free or carry
a nominal fee. Check your bank's fee schedule for exact amounts.

Q48: How can I increase my card's daily limit?
A: Contact your bank's call center, visit a branch, or use the mobile app
if the bank offers limit management. Limit increases are subject to bank
approval and may depend on your account type and history.

Q49: Are there fees for international transactions?
A: Yes. International transactions typically incur a currency conversion fee
of 1–3% above the exchange rate, and foreign ATM withdrawal fees of 2–3%
plus a fixed fee. Check your bank's international fee schedule.

Q50: What is the minimum balance required on a card account?
A: This varies by bank and account type. Some accounts require a minimum
balance of 10,000–50,000 UZS. Others have no minimum balance requirement.
Check with your specific bank.

ADDITIONAL FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q51: Can I use my Uzbekistan card on international websites like Amazon?
A: You need a Visa or Mastercard issued in Uzbekistan for international
websites. UzCard and Humo cards cannot be used for international online
purchases. Some virtual Visa cards issued by Uzbekistan banks work on
international platforms.

Q52: What should I do before traveling abroad with my bank card?
A: Notify your bank of your travel dates and destinations to prevent your card
from being blocked due to unusual foreign activity. Ensure your Visa or
Mastercard is enabled for international use and check the foreign transaction fees.

Q53: What is the Payme payment system?
A: Payme is a widely used Uzbek digital payment platform that allows users to
pay utility bills, make transfers, pay at merchants, and shop online. It requires
a linked UzCard or Humo card.

Q54: What is the Click payment system?
A: Click is another popular Uzbek digital payment platform similar to Payme,
offering bill payments, transfers, and mobile commerce services.

Q55: Can minors have a bank card in Uzbekistan?
A: Yes. Children aged 14 and above can open their own account and receive a
card. Children under 14 may receive supplementary cards under parental account
with parental consent.

Q56: What is an overdraft?
A: An overdraft allows you to spend more than your account balance, up to an
approved limit. The overdrawn amount is essentially a short-term loan that
accrues interest. Overdraft must be applied for and approved by the bank.

Q57: Can I use my card to pay utility bills in Uzbekistan?
A: Yes. Through mobile apps like Payme or Click, or directly via some banks'
mobile banking apps, you can pay electricity, gas, water, internet, and other
utility bills using your linked card.

Q58: What is a salary card (korporativ karta)?
A: A salary card is a debit card issued to an employee by the employer's bank
for receiving salary payments. The employer's company has an agreement with
the bank to disburse salaries directly to these cards.

Q59: What is the difference between UzCard and Humo?
A: Both are national domestic payment systems in Uzbekistan. UzCard was
established in 2004 and is more widely distributed. Humo was established in
2019 with more modern infrastructure. Since 2021, both systems are
interoperable — you can use either card at any domestic ATM or POS terminal.

Q60: What do I do if I receive a suspicious call asking for my card details?
A: Hang up immediately. Your bank will never ask for your full card number,
PIN, CVV, or OTP over the phone. Call your bank on the official number to
report the suspicious call. Do not call back numbers from unknown callers.

Q61: What is a card statement?
A: A card statement is a summary of all transactions made with your card over
a specific period (usually monthly). It shows credits, debits, fees, and the
current balance. Available via mobile app or at the branch.

Q62: Can I pay taxes using my bank card in Uzbekistan?
A: Yes. Tax payments can be made through the official online tax portal
(taxes.uz) and through major mobile banking platforms using a linked bank card.

Q63: What is contactless payment limit in Uzbekistan?
A: The contactless payment limit (without PIN) is generally set between
200,000 and 500,000 UZS. Transactions above this threshold require PIN entry.
Exact limits depend on the issuing bank and may be adjustable.

Q64: How do I link my card to Payme?
A: Open the Payme app, go to Cards, click Add Card, enter your 16-digit card
number, expiration date, and the OTP sent to your registered number. The card
will be linked and ready to use.

Q65: What happens if I receive a refund on a cancelled card?
A: The refund is directed to the bank account associated with the cancelled
card. Since the account usually remains active even after card cancellation,
the funds will be credited to the account and accessible via your new card or
at the branch.

Q66: What is an installment card (rassrochka)?
A: An installment card allows you to purchase goods and pay in equal monthly
installments without interest (or at reduced interest) over a set period
(e.g., 3, 6, or 12 months). Popular in Uzbekistan for electronics and
household appliance purchases.

Q67: How do I know if my card is enabled for international online payments?
A: Check your card settings in the mobile banking app under the card details
or settings section. You can enable or disable international online payments there.
Alternatively, call your bank's customer service.

Q68: Can I receive international money transfers to my Uzbekistan card?
A: Yes, through international transfer services like Western Union, MoneyGram,
and international SWIFT transfers. For SWIFT, you'll need your bank's SWIFT code
and your account number. Some banks also support transfers via Visa Direct
or Mastercard Send.

Q69: What is a QR payment?
A: QR payment allows you to pay by scanning a QR code displayed by the merchant
using your mobile banking app. The payment is processed instantly without
needing to enter card details manually.

Q70: Is mobile banking safe for card management?
A: Yes, when the official app is downloaded from the bank's official channels
(App Store or Google Play). Always use a strong password, enable biometric
login, keep your app updated, and do not use banking apps on jailbroken or
rooted devices.

Q71: What is a bank's acquiring service?
A: Bank acquiring refers to the bank service that allows merchants to accept
card payments through POS terminals and online payment gateways. The bank
that provides this service to the merchant is called the acquirer.

Q72: Can I set spending limits on my card?
A: Many Uzbekistan banks allow you to set custom daily spending limits through
the mobile banking app, lower than the bank's maximum allowed limit. This is
a security feature.

Q73: What is a foreign currency account?
A: A foreign currency account allows you to hold and transact in foreign
currencies (USD, EUR, etc.). Visa and Mastercard cards linked to such accounts
can be used for international transactions without currency conversion charges
(as the account is already in the foreign currency).

Q74: Can I use my bank card at online gambling or betting sites?
A: Some banks block transactions to gambling or betting merchants at the card
level. Additionally, Uzbekistan regulations may restrict certain categories
of online transactions. Attempting such transactions may lead to card flags.

Q75: What should I do if an ATM gives me counterfeit bills?
A: Note the ATM location, ID number, time, and transaction reference. Contact
your bank immediately. Do not try to use the counterfeit notes. Report to
local law enforcement if necessary.

SECTION 12: GLOSSARY OF BANKING CARD TERMS
================================================================================

Account: A formal banking arrangement in which a bank holds funds on behalf of
a customer.

Acquirer: A bank or financial institution that processes card transactions on
behalf of a merchant.

ATM (Automated Teller Machine): A machine that allows customers to perform
banking operations such as cash withdrawal, balance inquiry, and fund transfers.

Authorization: The process by which the issuing bank approves or declines a
card transaction.

Balance: The amount of money available in a bank account.

Card Number: The 16-digit number embossed on the front of a bank card that
uniquely identifies the card account.

Cardholder: The person in whose name a bank card is issued.

Chargeback: A forced reversal of a card transaction initiated by the issuing
bank at the cardholder's request following a dispute.

Chip (EMV): An embedded microchip on a bank card that provides secure data
storage and enables encrypted transaction processing.

Contactless: A payment method using NFC technology to make payments by tapping
the card near a reader.

Credit Limit: The maximum amount a cardholder can borrow on a credit card.

CVV/CVC: Card Verification Value/Code — a 3-digit security number on the back
of the card used for online and phone transactions.

Debit Card: A card that deducts funds directly from the linked bank account.

EMV: Europay, Mastercard, Visa — the global standard for chip-based payment
cards.

Expiration Date: The date printed on the card (MM/YY) after which the card is
no longer valid.

Fraud: Unauthorized use of a bank card or account for financial gain.

Humo: Uzbekistan's domestic payment card system launched in 2019.

Installment Card: A credit card that allows purchases to be paid in equal
monthly installments.

Issuer: The bank or financial institution that issues a card to a cardholder.

Merchant: A business or individual that accepts card payments for goods or services.

NFC (Near Field Communication): Wireless technology enabling contactless payments.

OTP (One-Time Password): A temporary password sent to a registered mobile number
for transaction verification.

Overdraft: A facility allowing a cardholder to spend more than the account balance
up to an approved limit.

PCI DSS: Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard — the global security
standard for card data protection.

PIN (Personal Identification Number): A secret code used to authenticate card
transactions at ATMs and POS terminals.

POS (Point of Sale): The location and device where a card payment is made at a
merchant.

Pre-authorization: A temporary hold placed on a card balance to reserve funds
for an expected charge.

Refund: The return of funds to a cardholder's account following a transaction reversal.

Settlement: The process by which funds are transferred from the cardholder's
account to the merchant's account after a transaction.

Skimming: A fraud technique using a device attached to an ATM or terminal to
steal card data.

Swift Code: An international bank identifier code used for international wire transfers.

UzCard: Uzbekistan's primary national payment card system established in 2004.

Virtual Card: A digital-only card with no physical form, used for online transactions.

3D Secure: A security protocol adding an extra authentication step for online card
transactions.

SECTION 14: REGULATORY AND COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

The banking card system in Uzbekistan operates under the oversight and regulation
of the following bodies:

Central Bank of the Republic of Uzbekistan (CBU):
- Issues regulations on payment systems and banking operations
- Oversees the stability and security of domestic payment infrastructure
- Establishes anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF)
  requirements for banks
- Sets consumer protection guidelines for banking services

Law on Payments and Payment Systems:
- Governs the legal framework for payment card operations in Uzbekistan
- Establishes rights and obligations of payment system participants
- Regulates dispute resolution mechanisms

Law on Banks and Banking Activity:
- Defines licensing and operational requirements for commercial banks
- Establishes the framework for card issuance and management

Consumer Protection:
- Cardholders in Uzbekistan have the right to file complaints with their bank
- If unresolved, complaints can be escalated to the Central Bank of Uzbekistan
- The CBU accepts consumer financial complaints through its official website
  and call center

Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance:
- Banks are required to monitor transactions for suspicious activity
- Large cash transactions above certain thresholds trigger reporting requirements
- Customers may be asked to provide documentation for large or unusual transactions
- Refusing to provide identification or explanation for suspicious transactions
  may result in account freezing pending investigation

Data Protection:
- Customer card data is protected under Uzbekistan's data protection regulations
- Banks are prohibited from sharing customer financial data with unauthorized
  third parties without consent
- PCI DSS compliance is required for all entities handling card data

SECTION 15: BEST PRACTICES FOR CARDHOLDERS
================================================================================

For everyday use:
1. Always keep your card in a safe, dedicated card slot — not loose in a bag.
2. Memorize your PIN — never write it down near the card.
3. Enable push notifications and SMS alerts for all transactions.
4. Check your bank statement at least once a week.
5. Sign the back of your card immediately upon receipt.
6. Activate your card promptly — do not leave it inactive for months.

For ATM use:
1. Inspect the ATM for any suspicious attachments before inserting your card.
2. Cover the keypad with your hand when entering your PIN.
3. Do not accept assistance from strangers at ATMs.
4. Collect your card before leaving the ATM — do not forget it in the slot.
5. Take your receipt and destroy it safely after checking.
6. Prefer ATMs inside bank branches or enclosed, monitored locations.

For online shopping:
1. Only shop on reputable, HTTPS-secured websites.
2. Use virtual cards or dedicated online payment cards for online purchases.
3. Never store card details in browsers or on unverified websites.
4. Always verify the OTP is for the transaction you intended to make.
5. Keep your mobile banking app updated.
6. Log out of mobile banking after use.

For travel:
1. Notify your bank before international travel to prevent fraud flags.
2. Carry at least two different payment methods (e.g., Visa and cash).
3. Know the emergency card block number for use abroad (most banks accept
   international calls on their customer service lines).
4. Keep a record of your card numbers and bank contact in a secure location
   separate from your wallet.

For receiving cards by mail:
1. Activate the card immediately upon receipt.
2. Set up your PIN before first use.
3. Destroy the PIN mailer after memorizing the PIN.
4. If the card is not received by the expected date, contact the bank.
